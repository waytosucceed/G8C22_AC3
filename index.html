The Hound of the Baskervilles-Part 2


Concepts we learnt today:

  • The story has strong connotations of gothic elements (horror and terror) embodied especially in the figure of the hellish hound.
  • The best evidence for the claim directly supports the assertion; comes from a source that is credible; and is not purely anecdotal or based on the personal experiences of a single individual or a small number of people.
  • A fact can be verified by observation and research. Opinions is something a person believes, considers, or feels. They cannot be proved. Thus certain claims do not have any evidence.
  • The climax of a story can have more than one high point.
