Idioms


A blessing in disguise: A good thing that seemed bad first
Use: The cancellation of my flight was a blessing in disguise because weather became unruly later.

Easy does it: Slow down
Use: This idiom is used by itself when you are advising someone to approach a task carefully and slowly. It is not used in sentences.

Context: When John was finishing his lunch too speedily on seeing his class teacher, the teacher looked at him and at once said “Easy does it.”
